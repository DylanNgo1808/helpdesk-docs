# How to Understand Draft Order Details

The draft order detail page gives you a complete view of a single draft order, including line items, payment summary, customer information, shipping and billing addresses, notes, and tags.

## Prerequisites

- Better PDF Invoice app installed on your Shopify store
- A Professional or Ultimate plan
- At least one draft order in your Shopify store

## Steps

### Step 1: Open a draft order

From the **Draft Orders** list, click on the draft order name (e.g., "#D1234") to open the detail page. The page title shows the draft order name.

{/* screenshot: Draft order detail page with the order name as the page title */}

### Step 2: Review line items in the Products section

The main section displays a **Products** table with columns: **Item**, **Quantity**, **Tax**, and **Total**. Each row shows the product thumbnail, product title, variant name (if not "Default Title"), unit price, and any discount applied.

{/* screenshot: Products table showing line items with thumbnails, variant names, prices, and quantities */}

### Step 3: Review the payment summary

Below the products table, the **Payment** card breaks down the order financials: **Subtotal** (with item count), **Discounts** (showing discount codes or "No discount"), **Shipping**, **Taxes** (with a "Show tax" link to view tax line details), and **Total**.

{/* screenshot: Payment summary card showing subtotal, discounts, shipping, taxes, and total */}

### Step 4: View tax details

If the draft order includes tax lines, click **Show tax** in the payment summary to open a modal showing each tax line with its title, rate, and amount, plus the total tax.

{/* screenshot: Tax details modal listing individual tax lines with rates and amounts */}

### Step 5: Check payment terms

If the draft order has payment terms set, a card below the payment summary displays the payment terms name (e.g., "Net 30") and the due date.

{/* screenshot: Payment terms card showing the due date and payment terms name */}

### Step 6: View customer information

The right sidebar shows the **Customer** card with the customer's name (linked to the Shopify admin customer page), **Contact Information** including email and phone number, and **Company** details if applicable.

{/* screenshot: Customer card in the right sidebar showing name, email, phone, and company */}

### Step 7: Check shipping and billing addresses

The customer card also displays the **Shipping Address** and **Billing Address**. If the billing address matches the shipping address, it shows "Same as shipping address."

{/* screenshot: Shipping and billing address sections in the customer card */}

### Step 8: Read notes and tags

Below the customer card, separate cards display the draft order **Notes** and **Tags**. Notes show any internal notes attached to the draft order. Tags are displayed as tag chips.

{/* screenshot: Notes card and Tags card showing draft order notes and Shopify tags */}

### Step 9: Use the action buttons

The page header contains action buttons: **Edit** (opens the draft order in Shopify admin for editing, only available for non-completed orders), **Print** (navigates to the print page), and **Download** (downloads the draft order as a PDF file).

{/* screenshot: Page header showing Edit, Print, and Download action buttons */}

### Step 10: Send a quote from the detail page

At the bottom of the payment summary, click the **Send Quote** button to open the send modal. This button is available only when the draft order status is not "Completed."

{/* screenshot: Send Quote button at the bottom of the payment summary */}

## Tips

- The **Edit** button opens the draft order directly in your Shopify admin, where you can modify line items, customer, and other details.
- Discounts applied at the order level (not line-item level) are displayed separately in the payment summary.
- If the customer has a B2B company associated, the company name and a link to the company page appear in the sidebar.
- Use the back arrow at the top left to return to the draft orders list.
