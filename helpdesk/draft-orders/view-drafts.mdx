# How to View and Navigate Draft Orders

The Draft Orders page displays all draft orders from your Shopify store. You can search, filter, sort, and paginate through your draft orders to find exactly what you need.

## Prerequisites

- Better PDF Invoice app installed on your Shopify store
- A Professional or Ultimate plan (draft orders require a paid plan)
- At least one draft order created in your Shopify admin

## Steps

### Step 1: Open the Draft Orders page

In the Better PDF Invoice app, click **Draft Orders** in the left navigation menu. The page title displays "Draft Orders" along with your plan badge if applicable.

{/* screenshot: Draft Orders page with the page title and navigation highlighted */}

### Step 2: Browse the draft orders table

The main table shows your draft orders with the following columns: **Draft Order** (name), **PO Number**, **Date**, **Customer**, **Total**, **Status**, and **Actions**.

{/* screenshot: Draft orders index table showing column headings and sample rows */}

### Step 3: Use status tabs to filter quickly

At the top of the table, click the status tabs to filter by common groupings: **All**, **Open & Invoice Sent**, **Open & Quote Sent**, **Open**, **Invoice Sent**, **Quote Sent**, or **Completed**.

{/* screenshot: Status tabs above the draft orders table */}

### Step 4: Search for a specific draft order

Type a draft order number, customer name, or keyword into the search bar at the top of the table to find specific draft orders. Results update automatically as you type.

{/* screenshot: Search bar with a search query entered and filtered results below */}

### Step 5: Apply advanced filters

Click the filter icon to access advanced filters. You can filter by **Status**, **Updated At**, **Created At**, **Customer**, **Source**, and **Tagged With**. Select your criteria and the table updates automatically.

{/* screenshot: Filter panel expanded showing Status, Updated At, Created At, Customer, Source, and Tagged With filters */}

### Step 6: Filter by date range

When filtering by **Created At** or **Updated At**, choose from preset ranges like **Today**, **In the last week**, **In the last month**, **In the last 3 months**, **In the last year**, or specify a custom date using **On or before** / **On or after**.

{/* screenshot: Date filter expanded showing preset date range options */}

### Step 7: Filter by customer

Expand the **Customer** filter to see a searchable dropdown. Type a customer name to search, then select the customer from the list. The dropdown supports scrolling and loads more results as needed.

{/* screenshot: Customer filter with search field and customer dropdown list */}

### Step 8: Sort draft orders

Use the sort dropdown to sort by **Draft Order** (oldest/newest) or **Price** (low to high / high to low). Click the sort direction arrow to reverse the order.

{/* screenshot: Sort dropdown showing sort options with direction toggles */}

### Step 9: View customer details

Click the expand arrow next to a customer name in the table to see a popover with the customer's full details: name, company, address, email (with a copy button), phone number, and order count. Click **View Customer** to open the customer in Shopify admin.

{/* screenshot: Customer popover showing contact details and View Customer button */}

### Step 10: Navigate between pages

Use the **Previous** and **Next** pagination buttons at the bottom of the table to navigate between pages of draft orders.

{/* screenshot: Pagination controls at the bottom of the draft orders table */}

### Step 11: View a note on a draft order

If a draft order has a note attached, a note icon appears next to the draft order name. Hover over the icon to see the note content in a tooltip.

{/* screenshot: Note icon next to a draft order name with tooltip visible */}

## Tips

- Draft orders with the status **Open** can still be edited in Shopify admin. **Completed** draft orders have been converted to orders.
- The **Invoice Sent** badge indicates the invoice has already been sent to the customer from Shopify.
- Use the **Tagged With** filter to search by Shopify tags applied to your draft orders.
- You can select multiple draft orders using the checkboxes to perform bulk actions like **Print** and **Download**.
