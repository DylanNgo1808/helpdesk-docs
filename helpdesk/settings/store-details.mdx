# How to Configure Your Store Details

Your store details appear on every PDF invoice you generate. This guide shows you how to set up your company name, address, phone, email, VAT number, and registered number so your invoices look professional and complete.

## Prerequisites

- Better PDF Invoice installed on your Shopify store
- Your business information ready (company name, address, contact details, tax numbers)

## Steps

### Step 1: Open Settings

From the Better PDF Invoice dashboard, click **Settings** in the app navigation. The Settings page will open with a left sidebar listing all settings sections.

{/* screenshot: Better PDF Invoice Settings page with the left navigation sidebar visible */}

### Step 2: Select Store Details

In the left sidebar, click **Store details**. This is the first item in the navigation list, indicated by a store icon. The Store details panel will appear on the right side.

{/* screenshot: Settings sidebar with "Store details" highlighted and the Store details panel visible on the right */}

### Step 3: Enter Your Company Name

In the **Information** section, locate the **Company name** field and enter your business name (e.g., "Avada Commerce"). This name will appear as the seller or issuer name on all your invoices.

{/* screenshot: Information section showing the "Company name" text field with an example value */}

### Step 4: Enter Your Address

Fill in the **Address** field with your full business address (e.g., "445 Mount Eden Road, Mount Eden, Auckland"). The address has a maximum length of 255 characters.

{/* screenshot: The "Address" text field with a placeholder showing "eg. 445 Mount Eden Road, Mount Eden, Auckland." */}

### Step 5: Add Your VAT Number

Enter your tax identification number in the **VAT number** field (e.g., "352522844"). This field is important for tax compliance and will display on your invoices under the store information section.

{/* screenshot: The "VAT number" field alongside the "Phone" field in a side-by-side layout */}

### Step 6: Enter Your Phone Number

Fill in the **Phone** field with your business phone number. This provides customers with a way to contact you directly from the invoice.

{/* screenshot: The "Phone" field with a placeholder showing "0000000000" */}

### Step 7: Add Your Email Address

Enter your business email in the **Email** field (e.g., "sales@domain.com"). This email address will appear as your contact email on invoices.

{/* screenshot: The "Email" field alongside the "Registered number" field in a side-by-side layout */}

### Step 8: Enter Your Registered Number

If your business has a registered company number, enter it in the **Registered number** field. This is optional but useful for businesses that need to display their registration details on invoices.

{/* screenshot: The "Registered number" field with a placeholder showing "xxxxxxxxxx" */}

### Step 9: Save Your Changes

Click the **Save** button in the save bar that appears at the top of the page. A success message "Save successfully" will confirm your changes have been applied.

{/* screenshot: The save bar at the top of the page with "Save" and "Discard" buttons, and the success toast */}

## Tips

- All store detail fields are optional, but filling them in makes your invoices look more professional and helps with tax compliance.
- Changes to store details apply to all new invoices generated after saving. Previously generated invoices are not affected.
- If you make a mistake, click the **Discard** button in the save bar to revert to the previous values before saving.
- You can also configure store details from the dashboard Quickstart by clicking the **Set up** button on the first task.
