# How to Choose and Customize Your Invoice Theme

Better PDF Invoice includes a gallery of professionally designed invoice themes. This guide walks you through browsing available themes, selecting one for your store, and customizing it to match your brand.

## Prerequisites

- Better PDF Invoice installed on your Shopify store
- Some themes require a Professional, Ultimate, or Wholesale plan

## Steps

### Step 1: Open the Templates Page

From the Better PDF Invoice app, click **Templates** in the app navigation. The Templates page displays all your current templates and available themes.

{/* screenshot: Better PDF Invoice navigation with "Templates" highlighted and the Templates page visible */}

### Step 2: Browse Template Types

If your store uses the new templates layout, you will see tabs at the top to filter templates by type:

- **Invoice** -- Standard invoice templates
- **Order** -- Order confirmation templates
- **Packing Slip** -- Packing slip templates
- **Refund** -- Refund document templates

Click a tab to view templates of that type.

{/* screenshot: Templates page showing the type tabs (Invoice, Order, Packing Slip, Refund) at the top */}

### Step 3: Review Available Themes

Each template is displayed as a card with a preview image, the template name, and action buttons. Available themes include:

- **Spring** -- Clean design with a warm color accent
- **Summer** -- Bright and modern layout
- **Autumn** -- Professional design with earth tones
- **Winter** -- Sleek, minimalist template
- **Paris** -- Elegant with strong lines and neat alignment
- **London** -- Professional, creation-inspired design
- **New York** -- Easy-to-read with colored content boxes
- **Venice** -- Elegant with well-proportioned columns
- **Sydney** -- Clean modern template
- **Barcelona** -- Versatile professional design
- **Elegance** -- Refined and sophisticated (Professional plan)
- **Lifestyle** -- Modern and stylish (Professional plan)
- **Tech** -- Contemporary tech-inspired design (Professional plan)
- **Professional** -- Business-focused layout (Professional plan)
- **CD Light** -- Light color scheme (Ultimate plan)
- **CD Dark** -- Dark color scheme (Ultimate plan)
- **POS** -- Receipt-style templates for point of sale (paper width: 80mm, 76mm, or 58mm)

{/* screenshot: Templates page showing a grid of template cards with preview images and names */}

### Step 4: Preview a Template

Click the **Preview** button on any template card to open a larger preview in a modal window. This lets you see the full layout, typography, and color scheme before selecting a theme.

{/* screenshot: Template preview modal showing a full-size view of a selected template */}

### Step 5: Select a Template

Click the **Select** button on a template card to mark it as your active template. The button will change to **Unselect**, indicating the template is now selected for use. You can select multiple templates for different purposes.

{/* screenshot: A template card with the "Select" button highlighted, and another card showing the "Unselect" state */}

### Step 6: Edit a Template

Click the **Edit** button on any template card to open the template editor. This takes you to a detailed editing page where you can customize the template.

{/* screenshot: A template card with the "Edit" button highlighted */}

### Step 7: Customize Colors

In the template editor, look for the **Color** option. Click it to open a color picker where you can change the accent color of your template to match your brand colors.

{/* screenshot: Template editor showing the color picker for changing the template accent color */}

### Step 8: Explore Theme Options in the Editor

The template editor allows you to load a completely different theme by clicking **Explore themes** or **Load theme**. This opens the theme gallery where you can browse all available designs and load one into the editor.

{/* screenshot: Template editor with the "Explore themes" or "Load theme" button highlighted */}

### Step 9: Upgrade for Premium Themes

Templates marked with a **Professional**, **Ultimate**, or **Wholesale** badge require a plan upgrade. Click the **Upgrade** button on the template card to navigate to the subscription page where you can upgrade your plan.

{/* screenshot: A template card showing a premium badge and the "Upgrade" button */}

### Step 10: Create a New Template (Classic Layout)

If your store uses the classic template layout, click the **New template** button at the top-right of the Templates page. Select a base theme from the gallery and give your template a name to create a new custom template.

{/* screenshot: The "New template" button at the top of the Templates page */}

### Step 11: Delete a Template (Classic Layout)

On the classic layout, click the **Delete** button on a template card to remove it. A confirmation modal will ask "Are you sure?" -- click **OK** to confirm the deletion.

{/* screenshot: The delete confirmation modal with "Are you sure?" message and OK/Cancel buttons */}

## Tips

- Start with a free theme like Spring, Summer, Autumn, or Winter to familiarize yourself with the template system before upgrading.
- POS templates are designed for thermal receipt printers and use narrower paper widths (58mm, 76mm, or 80mm).
- You can have multiple templates active at the same time and choose which one to use when printing or downloading invoices.
- The "Want to customize your template?" banner at the top of the page connects you with the support team if you need help with advanced customization.
- Use the **Preview** button to compare different themes before selecting one.
