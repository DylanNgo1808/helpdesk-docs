# How to Upload and Manage Your Store Logo

Your store logo appears on every PDF invoice and email you send through Avada PDF Invoice. This guide shows you how to upload your logo, adjust its dimensions for both PDFs and emails, and remove or replace it when needed.

## Prerequisites

- Avada PDF Invoice installed on your Shopify store
- A logo image file in a supported format (PNG, JPG, or GIF)
- A paid plan (logo upload is available on paid plans)

## Steps

### Step 1: Open Settings

From the Avada PDF Invoice app, click **Settings** in the app navigation to open the Settings page.

{/* screenshot: Avada PDF Invoice navigation with "Settings" highlighted */}

### Step 2: Navigate to Store Details

In the left sidebar of the Settings page, click **Store details**. The Store details panel will open, showing the **Logo** section at the top.

{/* screenshot: Settings page with "Store details" selected and the Logo section visible */}

### Step 3: Upload Your Logo

In the **Logo** section, you can upload your logo in two ways:

- **Drag and drop** your logo file directly onto the drop zone area.
- Click the **Upload logo** button to open a file picker and select your image.

The app accepts image files (PNG, JPG, GIF).

{/* screenshot: The Logo section showing the DropZone area with the upload button and a placeholder thumbnail */}

### Step 4: Preview Your Logo

After uploading, your logo will appear in the thumbnail preview next to the upload area. Verify that the logo looks correct and is the right image.

{/* screenshot: The Logo section showing the uploaded logo in the thumbnail preview */}

### Step 5: Adjust PDF Logo Height

In the **Logo height** field below the upload area, enter a value in pixels to control the height of your logo on PDF invoices. If left empty, the default value is **75** pixels.

{/* screenshot: The "Logo height" field with the help text "If empty, default value: 75" */}

### Step 6: Adjust PDF Logo Width

In the **Logo width** field, enter a value in pixels to control the width of your logo on PDF invoices. If left empty, the default value is **150** pixels. The maximum value is 1000 pixels.

{/* screenshot: The "Logo width" field next to the "Logo height" field in a side-by-side layout */}

### Step 7: Set Email Logo Dimensions

If you want your logo to appear at a different size in emails, adjust the **Email logo height** and **Email logo width** fields. The default values are 75px height and 150px width. These settings only affect logos in invoice emails, not PDF documents.

{/* screenshot: The "Email logo height" and "Email logo width" fields in a side-by-side layout */}

### Step 8: Save Your Changes

Click the **Save** button in the save bar at the top of the page to apply your logo and dimension settings.

{/* screenshot: The save bar with "Save" button highlighted */}

### Step 9: Replace Your Logo

To replace your logo with a new one, click the **Edit** button next to the logo thumbnail. Select a new image file, and the logo will be updated.

{/* screenshot: The Logo section with the "Edit" button highlighted next to the current logo thumbnail */}

### Step 10: Remove Your Logo

To remove your logo entirely, click the **Remove** button (appears in red next to the Edit button). The logo will be cleared and replaced with a default placeholder image.

{/* screenshot: The Logo section showing the "Edit" and "Remove" buttons next to the logo thumbnail */}

### Step 11: Verify on an Invoice

To confirm your logo appears correctly, navigate to the **Orders** page, select an order, and preview or print the invoice. Your uploaded logo should display at the top of the invoice at the dimensions you specified.

{/* screenshot: A PDF invoice preview showing the uploaded store logo at the top of the document */}

## Tips

- For best results, use a logo with a transparent background (PNG format) so it blends well with any invoice template.
- Keep your logo file size under 2MB to ensure fast upload and rendering.
- If your logo appears stretched or distorted, try adjusting only one dimension (height or width) and leaving the other empty to maintain the aspect ratio.
- The logo settings under Store details apply globally. You can also upload a different logo per template in the template editor.
