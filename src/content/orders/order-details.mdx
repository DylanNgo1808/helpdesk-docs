# How to Understand the Order Detail View

The order detail page shows the full breakdown of a single order, including fulfillment status, line items, payment information, customer details, and available actions like printing, downloading, and sending invoices.

## Prerequisites

- Avada PDF Invoice app installed on your Shopify store
- At least one order synced from your Shopify store

## Steps

### Step 1: Open an Order

From the **Orders** page, click on an order number (e.g., #1001) to open its detail page. The page title shows the order name with status badges for payment and fulfillment.

{/* screenshot: Order detail page header with order name and status badges */}

### Step 2: Review the Page Subtitle

Below the order name, a subtitle shows "Last updated on [date] from [location]" indicating when the order was last modified and whether it originated from the **Online Store** or **Draft Orders**.

{/* screenshot: Order subtitle showing last updated date and source */}

### Step 3: View Fulfillment Sections

The main content area displays fulfillment cards for each fulfillment group. Each card shows the fulfillment status (Fulfilled, Unfulfilled, On hold, Fulfillment in progress) along with the line items, quantities, and pricing.

{/* screenshot: Fulfillment card showing line items with quantities and prices */}

### Step 4: Review Payment Information

The **Payment** section shows the order's financial summary including **Subtotal**, **Shipping**, **Taxes**, **Discounts**, and **Total**. Click **Show discounts**, **Show shipping**, or **Show tax rates** to expand those details.

{/* screenshot: Payment section with subtotal, shipping, taxes, and total */}

### Step 5: View Tax Details

Click **Show tax rates** in the Payment section to open a modal displaying individual tax lines with their rates and amounts.

{/* screenshot: Tax details modal showing tax line breakdown */}

### Step 6: Review Customer Information

In the right sidebar, find the **Customer** section showing the customer name and contact information. Below that, the **Shipping address** and **Billing address** sections display the order's delivery and billing details. The section also shows **Notes**, **PO number**, and **Tags**.

{/* screenshot: Right sidebar with customer info, shipping address, billing address, notes, and tags */}

### Step 7: Use the Print Action

Click the **Print** button in the secondary actions bar at the top of the page to navigate to the print preview page for this order.

{/* screenshot: Secondary actions bar with Print button highlighted */}

### Step 8: Use the Download Action

Click the **Download** button in the secondary actions bar to download the order as a PDF file directly to your device.

{/* screenshot: Secondary actions bar with Download button highlighted */}

### Step 9: Send an Invoice

Click **Send invoice** to open the send modal where you can specify the recipient email, choose an invoice template, select a language, and send the invoice via email.

{/* screenshot: Send invoice modal with email, template, and language fields */}

### Step 10: Edit the Order in Shopify

Click **Edit** in the secondary actions bar to open the order editing page in your Shopify admin in a new browser tab.

{/* screenshot: Secondary actions bar with Edit button highlighted */}

### Step 11: Access Combine Orders (B2B)

For B2B orders with payment terms and a company, a **Combine orders** button appears in the secondary actions. Click it to navigate to the company's B2B page where you can combine multiple orders into one invoice.

{/* screenshot: Combine orders button in secondary actions for a B2B order */}

### Step 12: Mark as Paid

For unpaid orders, a **Mark as paid** option allows you to manually record a payment. Click it to open a confirmation modal showing the amount to mark as paid.

{/* screenshot: Mark as paid confirmation modal with amount displayed */}

## Tips

- The order detail page displays banners for special situations, such as when an order has been updated and needs the invoice resent, or when a benefit period has expired.
- Payment terms information (issue date, due date, payment terms type) appears for B2B orders with configured payment terms.
- Navigate back to the orders list using the back arrow in the top-left corner of the page.
