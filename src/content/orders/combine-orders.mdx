# How to Combine Multiple Orders into One Invoice

Combining orders merges multiple separate orders into a single combined order with a unified invoice. This is especially useful for B2B customers who place multiple orders and need one consolidated invoice.

## Prerequisites

- Better PDF Invoice app installed on your Shopify store
- Wholesale plan subscription
- At least two orders from the same company with payment terms
- Orders must be open (not closed, canceled, or fully paid)

## Steps

### Step 1: Navigate to the B2B Page

From the order detail page of a B2B order, click **Combine orders** in the secondary actions bar. Alternatively, navigate to the B2B section from the app sidebar.

{/* screenshot: Combine orders button on a B2B order detail page */}

### Step 2: Select Orders to Combine

On the company orders page, select two or more orders from the same company by checking the checkboxes next to each order.

{/* screenshot: Company orders page with multiple orders selected */}

### Step 3: Initiate the Combine

Click the **Combine orders** button in the bulk actions. The app navigates to the Combine Orders page showing all selected orders.

{/* screenshot: Combine orders bulk action button */}

### Step 4: Review the Combined Fulfillments

The main content area shows a **Fulfillments** section listing all line items from the selected orders grouped by their fulfillment status (e.g., "Unfulfilled"). Review the items, quantities, and pricing.

{/* screenshot: Combined fulfillments section showing line items from multiple orders */}

### Step 5: Configure Payment Terms

The **Payment terms** section shows the payment due configuration. Select the payment terms type, issue date, and due date for the combined order.

{/* screenshot: Payment terms section with type, issue date, and due date fields */}

### Step 6: Add Shipping

In the **Payment** section, click **Add shipping** to open the shipping modal. Choose from two options:
- **Order shipping fee** -- select a shipping rate from one of the original orders
- **Custom shipping fee** -- enter a custom shipping name and price

{/* screenshot: Add shipping modal with Order shipping fee and Custom shipping fee options */}

### Step 7: Configure Custom Shipping (Optional)

If choosing custom shipping, enter a **Name** (e.g., "Free ship") and a **Price** in the currency field, then click **Add shipping** to apply.

{/* screenshot: Custom shipping fee form with name and price fields */}

### Step 8: Review the Payment Summary

The Payment section shows the combined **Subtotal**, **Discount** (merged from all orders), **Shipping**, **Taxes**, and **Total**. Click **Show tax rate** to view the tax breakdown.

{/* screenshot: Payment summary showing subtotal, discount, shipping, taxes, and total */}

### Step 9: Select a Customer

In the right sidebar, use **Select customer** to assign a customer to the combined order. The customer's contact information, shipping address, and billing address are populated automatically.

{/* screenshot: Right sidebar with customer selection and address information */}

### Step 10: Edit Addresses (Optional)

Click **Edit shipping address** or **Edit billing address** in the right sidebar to modify the shipping or billing information for the combined order.

{/* screenshot: Edit shipping address modal with address form fields */}

### Step 11: Create the Combined Order

Click **Create combined order** at the bottom of the Payment section. A confirmation modal appears explaining: "This action will merge the selected orders into this single order. The original selected orders will be canceled."

{/* screenshot: Create combined order confirmation modal */}

### Step 12: Confirm and Submit

Click **Create combined order** in the confirmation modal to finalize. A success toast "Combine successfully" confirms the action, and you are redirected to the new combined order's detail page.

{/* screenshot: Success toast after combined order is created */}

## Tips

- You can combine up to 10 orders at a time.
- Orders must have payment terms and be from the same company to be combined.
- The original orders are canceled after combining (unless the "Cancel original order" setting is disabled in your app settings).
- Discount information from all original orders is automatically merged and displayed in the combined order.
- You can modify or remove shipping after adding it by clicking the shipping line to open the edit or remove modal.
