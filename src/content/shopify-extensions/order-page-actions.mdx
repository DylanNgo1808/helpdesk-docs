# How to Use Print, Download, and Send Actions on the Order Detail Page

Avada PDF Invoice adds three action links to every order detail page in Shopify Admin: **Print order by Avada**, **Download order by Avada**, and **Send mail by Avada**. These let you generate, save, or email a PDF invoice without leaving the order page.

## Prerequisites

- Avada PDF Invoice app installed and active on your Shopify store
- At least one order in your store
- An active subscription plan that supports printing (free plan has limited access)

## Steps

### Step 1: Open an Order in Shopify Admin

Navigate to **Orders** in your Shopify Admin sidebar. Click on any order to open its detail page.

{/* screenshot: Shopify Admin sidebar with Orders highlighted */}

### Step 2: Locate the "More actions" Menu

On the order detail page, find the **More actions** button in the top-right area of the page. Click it to reveal a dropdown menu with available action links.

{/* screenshot: Order detail page showing the "More actions" dropdown button */}

### Step 3: Print an Invoice Using "Print order by Avada"

Click **Print order by Avada** from the dropdown menu. The app opens a print preview page where you can:

- **Select a template** -- Choose one or more invoice templates from the template checklist on the right sidebar.
- **Choose a language** -- Use the language dropdown to select a language for the invoice. Language selection requires a Pro plan or higher.
- **Preview the PDF** -- The left panel displays a live PDF preview of the invoice rendered with your selected template and language.

Click the **Download** button in the top-right corner to save the PDF to your computer, or use your browser's print dialog to send it to a printer.

{/* screenshot: Print preview page with template selector, language dropdown, and PDF preview */}

### Step 4: Download an Invoice Using "Download order by Avada"

Click **Download order by Avada** from the More actions menu. The app immediately generates and downloads the PDF invoice file to your computer using your default template. A loading indicator appears while the file is being prepared.

{/* screenshot: Download in progress with loading indicator */}

### Step 5: Send an Invoice via Email Using "Send mail by Avada"

Click **Send mail by Avada** from the More actions menu. The app automatically sends the invoice as a PDF attachment to the customer's email address associated with the order. A success notification appears confirming the email was sent.

{/* screenshot: Success toast notification showing "Email sent successfully" */}

### Step 6: Change Template on the Print Preview Page

On the print preview page (opened via Print order by Avada), the right sidebar shows a list of all your Order-type templates with checkboxes. Select or deselect templates to include multiple invoices in a single PDF. The preview updates automatically.

{/* screenshot: Template checklist on the right sidebar with multiple templates */}

### Step 7: Change the Invoice Language

On the print preview page, use the **Choose language** dropdown above the template list to switch the invoice language. If you are on the Free plan, this dropdown prompts you to upgrade. On Pro plans and above, you can choose from all supported languages.

{/* screenshot: Language dropdown showing available languages */}

### Step 8: Navigate Back to the Order

Click the **Back** arrow button in the top-left of the print preview page, or click **View order** in the secondary action bar to return to the order detail page in Shopify Admin.

{/* screenshot: Back button and View order link on the print preview page */}

## Tips

- The **Download** action uses your default template and primary language, making it the fastest way to get a PDF.
- The **Print** action gives you the most control, letting you select templates and language before generating the PDF.
- The **Send** action emails the invoice to the customer using your configured email template and delivery settings.
- If you see an "Upgrade required" message, your current plan does not support printing for this order. Visit the Subscription page to upgrade.
- You can manage which templates appear as defaults by editing template settings and toggling the **Default for printing** option.
